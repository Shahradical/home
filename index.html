What is a Capybara?

The Capybara is a large, rat-like creature with a short head and neck, as well as a brown fur all over its body. A unique feature of the Capybara is that its sweat glands are located near the surface of its skin, unlike other rodents in its family.

Ecology

Capybara's are semiaquatic animals that usually reside in most countries in South America, Excluding Chile. As such, they usually live in dense forest areas or, being the excellent swimmers they are; near bodies of water. Some examples are swamps, lagoons, ponds, marshes and some areas of wet tropical rainforest. They can hold their breath for up to five minutes.

Diet

Capybara's are herbivores. This means that most of their diets involve plants, such as grass and fruits;however, they are known to be extremely picky. During the colder months of the year they prefer to eat more nutritious roots over the food they usually eat in the summer.
